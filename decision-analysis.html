Decision Science Frameworks
Four evidence-based tools that surface what pure logic misses — gut truth, regret risk, and hidden assumptions. The most useful signal is when gut and analysis disagree: that's where to dig.
Gut Check
You wake up tomorrow — the decision is made. Rate your visceral reaction to each city before your brain starts justifying.
Regret Minimisation — Bezos Framework
You are 80. Looking back at this moment — how much would you regret not having chosen each city? High regret score = you probably want it more than you admit.
Pre-Mortem — Worst Case
Pick your current frontrunner. 18 months later — the move was a mistake. What specifically went wrong? Surfaces risks that optimism hides.

Gut vs. Logic — Alignment Check
Agreement across all three = high confidence. A conflict on any axis means dig deeper — your gut may know something the numbers don't, or fear may be distorting your gut reading.
Pairwise Comparison
Sometimes the weighted ranking feels wrong. Force binary choices head-to-head to surface preferences the numbers miss. This is especially useful when two cities are close in score.
